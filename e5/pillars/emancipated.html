Emancipated

The unfinished obligations of freedom. Documented obligations become policy.

Emancipation created obligations. Those obligations were broken. The emancipated pillar of E5 is built on a single premise: the obligations of 1865 are not historical. They are current. The Freedmen's Bureau was abolished before it could fulfill its mandate. The promise of 40 acres was rescinded. The debt did not die when the creditors were ignored. It compounded.

Restitution 246 is a disciplined wage-loss and opportunity-loss framework for the 246 years of stolen labor — built on Lloyd Kelly's Five Questions and anchored to the Craemer (2015) wage-hour methodology. It does not claim to settle the full reparations question. It is one disciplined lane — irrefutable, citable, and ready for policy.
